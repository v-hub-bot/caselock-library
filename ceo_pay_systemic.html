🗺️ All 8 States Aqua Serves — The Same Pattern Everywhere

Colleen Arnold Oversees All of This. Franklin Signs Off on All of It.

Aqua delivers water and wastewater service to approximately 5.5 million people across these 8 states. In every state, the same pattern repeats: rate increases filed, regulators approve, quality complaints mount, executives get paid. Here is what is documented in each state.

🏛️ Pennsylvania — Largest Market
Regulator: Pennsylvania Public Utility Commission (PUC)
Active Rate Fight
What Aqua filed: A $126.7 million annual rate increase request for water and wastewater operations — one of the largest in state history.
What the PUC approved (Feb 7, 2025): "Significantly lower than requested" — but still approved increases. A residential customer using 3,870 gallons in Rate Zone 1 saw their bill go from $80.91 to $90.86 per month — an increase described as partial. In 2022, rates went up 10% for water and 50% for wastewater.
Prior rate history: Aqua received a rate increase in 2022. Filed again in 2024/2025.
Consumer advocacy: Pennsylvania Office of Consumer Advocate filed a formal complaint against the proposed rate increase with the PUC. PA State Senator Comitta reminded residents of opportunities to oppose the increase.
Community response: Reddit (r/Delco) active with resident complaints about rising bills. Facebook groups documenting customer frustration.
Source: PUC press release Feb 7, 2025 (puc.pa.gov); 6ABC Philadelphia; PA Senate Comitta statement; Aqua Pennsylvania rate page.
🏛️ Virginia — Caroline County Crisis
Regulator: Virginia State Corporation Commission (SCC)
Documented Water Quality Failures
What Aqua filed: 33.88% water revenue increase + 21.08% wastewater revenue increase (SCC Case PUR-2025-00071).
Rate history: 8 rate increases filed since 2006. SCC approved every single one — 8 for 8.
What residents experience: VDH-confirmed elevated iron and manganese. Filters saturate in 30 days. Children developed eczema requiring prescription treatment. Three holiday service failures in 2025. Complaint counts in SCC records dropped from 38 to 3 despite hundreds of documented community reports.
April 28, 2026 SCC hearing: Aqua customers lambasted the SCC directly. WTVR reported: "Aqua customers lambaste state regulator over poor water quality, high costs."
What residents pay: $160–$200+ per month for water they cannot safely drink or bathe children in.
Source: SCC Case PUR-2025-00071; Caroline County BOS agenda (33.88% confirmed); WTVR April 28, 2026; VDH findings.

What Virginia Law Currently Permits

1
Legislators may own stock in utility companies AND vote on legislation that increases their personal wealth. No prohibition. Legal under current Virginia law. Documented by Virginia Conservation Network.
2
Utilities may donate unlimited amounts directly to the legislators who regulate them. No cap. No restriction on amount. Virginia utilities have donated $27.7 million since 1996.
3
Legislators may use campaign donations for personal expenses — mortgage, car, tuition. No prohibition on personal use of campaign funds. Virginia Conservation Network documented this explicitly.
4
Those same legislators vote to appoint SCC commissioners who then regulate the utilities that funded their campaigns. Energy and Policy Institute documented this directly when Bagot and Towell were appointed in January 2024.
5
Two Virginia lawmakers proposed a bill to ban utility donations to legislators. A Democrat and a Republican both proposed it — because the conflict is that obvious. It didn't pass. (WRIC News documented.)
