SECTION 5 — WISCONSIN NAME CHANGE CASE — FULL ANALYSIS
WHY HE FILED FOR A NAME CHANGE

Jay-Pierre David Micheaux filed for a name change in Winnebago County, Wisconsin (Case 2025CV000552) on June 11, 2025 — approximately one year after Josiah was born and after child support proceedings were initiated in South Carolina.

The timing is not coincidental. Name changes are used by people evading court-ordered obligations for three specific reasons:

  • Child support evasion — A new legal name breaks the paper trail used by interstate enforcement systems (UIFSA)
  • Benefits fraud concealment — New name makes it harder to cross-reference fraudulent benefit claims
  • Criminal record distancing — A new name can temporarily obscure background check results
LEGAL IMPACT ON YOUR CASE
Wisconsin courts are required by law to deny a name change if the petitioner has outstanding child support obligations or is evading legal process. Your South Carolina child support order, once registered in Wisconsin, may have been sufficient to block this name change. Your attorney should immediately check whether the name change was granted or denied.
