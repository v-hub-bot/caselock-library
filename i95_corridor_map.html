Ashburn, Virginia (Loudoun County) is now called "Data Center Alley" and contains more data center square footage than anywhere else on earth. Here is what that looks like:

  • Loudoun County processes approximately 70% of the world's internet traffic through its data center infrastructure
  • Over 300 data centers concentrated in a single county
  • Loudoun County has seen explosive property tax revenue — but also massive strain on water, power, and road infrastructure
  • Dulles Greenway corridor has essentially been consumed by industrial data center campuses — residential character permanently altered
  • The industry has now saturated Loudoun and is actively moving south along I-95 and west into Culpeper and Fauquier — exactly what Richard Kuser's map documents
  • Tony Maccini's warning about Broad Run and Goose Creek reflects Loudoun's actual experience: local water sources are already under severe stress from Ashburn's data centers

⚠️ The Rust Belt Warning — "Move In, Drain, and Leave"

Christina Lhamon's Warning Is Historically Documented

Christina Lhamon's comment — "Just like the rust belt...move in, drain, and leave" — is not just a slogan. It describes a documented economic pattern:

  • Steel mills in Pittsburgh, Gary, Youngstown: Moved in with promises of jobs and tax revenue. Extracted value for decades. Left when costs rose elsewhere. Communities left with contaminated land, depleted infrastructure, and no tax base.
  • Coal in Appalachia (including Wyoming County, WV): Same pattern. Extracted resource, employed locals at below-market wages, left when seams ran out. Communities still paying for remediation 40 years later.
  • Data centers apply the same model to water and power: They need cheap land, cheap water, cheap power, and favorable tax treatment. Rural impoverished communities offer all four. When better deals emerge elsewhere — or when local water runs out — the infrastructure is not portable, but the company is.
  • The key difference from previous extraction: Steel mills left contaminated soil. Data centers will leave a depleted aquifer. You cannot remediate a dry aquifer on a 40-year timeline. The Eastern Virginia aquifer system is projected to reach depletion by 2055 — within the lifespan of children living in these communities today.
Christina Lhamon is correct about one specific thing: Bezos, Google, and Microsoft executives do not live in Caroline County, Spotsylvania County, or Wyoming County, WV. When the Rappahannock runs dry or the aquifer depletes, their homes are not affected. Their children do not drink this water. Their property values are not impacted. The externalized cost lands entirely on the people who remain.
Historical rust belt documentation: Brookings Institution; Ohio History Central; Appalachian Citizens' Law Center. Christina Lhamon community testimony attributed as personal opinion — not independently verified as a factual claim about specific companies' intentions.
