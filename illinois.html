SECTION 2 — ILLINOIS LAW IS ON YOUR SIDE
WHAT ILLINOIS GIVES YOU THAT OTHER STATES DON'T
✅ KEY FACT: Illinois has some of the strongest local zoning authority in the Midwest. Your town's zoning board and village/city council have real power here — more than they probably realize.
LEGAL TOOLWHAT IT DOESHOW TO USE IT
Illinois Municipal Code — 65 ILCS 5/11-13Gives municipalities full authority to regulate land use, including industrial facilities, through zoning ordinancesDemand the board produce the current zoning classification for the proposed site. If it's not already zoned heavy industrial, they need a variance — and variances require public hearings and can be denied
Special Use PermitsData centers often require a Special Use Permit in residential or light commercial zones. These require separate public hearings and findingsAsk at the meeting: "Has a Special Use Permit application been filed? If so, when is the public hearing?"
Illinois Environmental Protection Act — 415 ILCS 5Requires environmental review for facilities with significant water use or air emissionsRequest that the board require a full Environmental Impact Assessment before any permits are issued
Illinois Open Meetings Act — 5 ILCS 120All deliberations on zoning and permits must be public. No secret votes, no closed sessions on this issueIf you find out decisions were made without proper notice, those decisions can be challenged and voided
Illinois Administrative Procedure ActAny permit issued without following proper procedure is subject to administrative appealDocument every step. If they skip a required step, you have grounds to challenge
Local Moratorium PowerIllinois municipalities can pass an emergency moratorium on new industrial permits while they study the impactsPropose this at the meeting — see the script in Section 4
THE TAX BREAK PROBLEM — ILLINOIS SB 2858
⚠️ CRITICAL: Illinois law currently gives data centers a full sales tax exemption on equipment purchases — worth hundreds of millions of dollars. This means the facility gets massive state subsidies while your town absorbs all the costs (roads, water, power, emergency services). Your town gets none of that tax money back. Say this at the meeting.

We're not against jobs — we're against a bad deal for our community."
SECTION 4 — THE MAY 26TH MEETING — EXACTLY WHAT TO SAY
BEFORE YOU SPEAK — DO THESE
  • Sign in when you arrive — your name goes on the official record
  • Ask to speak during the public comment period — you have a legal right to speak
  • Bring this page on your phone or printed — reference specific laws by name
  • Bring neighbors — numbers matter even when the vote seems set
YOUR OFFICIAL PUBLIC COMMENT — READ THIS WORD FOR WORD
🎤 Public comment script: "My name is [name] and I'm a resident of [town]. I'm here to formally object to the proposed data center development and to request that this board take the following specific actions before any permits are issued. First — I request confirmation of the current zoning classification for this site and whether a Special Use Permit is required. If a variance or special use permit is needed, that process requires its own separate public hearing. Second — I request that this board require a full Environmental Impact Assessment covering water draw, noise levels at residential property lines, traffic, and emergency response capacity before any approval. Third — I formally propose that this board adopt a 90-day moratorium on new heavy industrial permits while an independent study of infrastructure capacity is conducted. This is a standard and legal action under Illinois municipal authority. Fourth — I want this board to disclose what tax incentives have been promised to this developer and what direct benefit this town's tax base receives in return. I am prepared to appeal any approval that does not follow proper zoning procedure under 65 ILCS 5/11-13 and the Illinois Environmental Protection Act. I am documenting this meeting and these proceedings. Thank you."
💡 WHY "I AM DOCUMENTING THIS" MATTERS: When you say that out loud at a public meeting, board members know that any procedural shortcuts are on the record. It changes how carefully they follow the rules.
QUESTIONS TO ASK FROM THE FLOOR
#QUESTION TO ASKWHY IT MATTERS
1"What is the current zoning classification of this parcel and what permits are required?"Forces them to state on record what process must be followed
2"Has an Environmental Impact Assessment been completed or required?"If the answer is no, that's a procedural gap you can challenge
3"How many gallons of water per day will this facility require and where does that water come from?"Most small towns have never modeled this — the answer will alarm people
4"What is the projected permanent employment at this facility?"Forces the "jobs" argument to be stated as a specific, verifiable number
5"What tax incentives are being offered and what is the net tax revenue to our town after those incentives?"Often the net number is near zero or negative for the first 10–20 years
6"Has our fire department been consulted on lithium battery fire response capacity?"Almost never done — and the answer exposes a real public safety gap
