📋 ACTION GUIDE

How to File Your SCC Complaint
Against Aqua Virginia

A phone call to the SCC does NOT count as a formal complaint. This guide tells you exactly what to file, what to include, and what language the SCC needs to hear — based on testimony from a resident who appeared at the April 28, 2026 hearing.

⚠️ Most Important Thing First

A phone call to the SCC saying "stop the rate hike" does NOT count as a formal complaint. The SCC needs your documented story — specific incidents, health impacts, appliance damage, water quality issues with details. Without a formal written complaint, you are invisible in the case record. File now while Case No. PUR-2025-00071 is still open. The final order has not been issued.

📦 What Your Complaint Must Include

The SCC Needs Receipts — Not Just Frustration

Based on testimony by Melanie Jean, who appeared at the April 28, 2026 SCC hearing and has direct knowledge of what the Commission requires to validate complaints and order refunds.

✅ Include: Appliance Damage

Water heater failures, dishwasher damage, washing machine deposits, grinder damage, plumbing discoloration. Repair receipts and replacement invoices are your strongest evidence. Jeff Black documented $5,000 in personal appliance damage over 3 years.

✅ Include: Water Quality Specifics

Brown water. Black slime. Chlorine values at your spigot. Sediment. Filter saturation dates and photos. Don't say "bad water" — say "brown water on [date], filter fully saturated after [X] days, photo attached."

✅ Include: Health Impacts

Eczema. Hair loss. Skin rashes. Itching after showering. Stomach issues. Name the condition. Name the family member affected. Include prescription or doctor visit documentation if you have it. VDH confirmed elevated iron and manganese in Aqua's water — that is an official government finding. Peer-reviewed research has linked elevated iron and manganese to skin conditions including eczema. Include your doctor's diagnosis and let the medical record speak for itself.

✅ Include: Financial Harm

Filter replacement costs. Bottled water purchases. Dermatologist visits. Special soaps and lotions (Dr. Teals, Aveeno, prescription creams). Bill increases. Every receipt is a damage number.

✅ Include: Service Failures

Memorial Day 2025 outage. Labor Day 2025 outage. Thanksgiving 2025 outage. May 22, 2026 pressure event — zero notification. Dates matter. Aqua's own Disruption Event Viewer confirmed the May 22 event.

✅ Include: Billing Issues

Unexpected bill increases before the final SCC order. Lump-sum retroactive charges. Unauthorized autopay withdrawals. Attach your bills. Compare month over month.

❌ What Does NOT Help

"Stop the rate hike" — not a complaint. "The water is bad" without specifics — not enough. A phone call — not a formal record. A Facebook post — not in the SCC docket. You must file in writing with specific documented incidents.

📮 How to File

Three Ways to Submit — All Count

1

Online — Fastest

Go to scc.virginia.gov/pages/utility-complaint-form
Select: Electric, Gas & Water Utilities → Water → Aqua Virginia
Reference Case No. PUR-2025-00071 in your complaint.
Attach photos, bills, receipts, and medical documentation if you have them.

2

Email — Direct to Case Staff

Email: scc-complaints@scc.virginia.gov
Subject line: Formal Complaint — Aqua Virginia — Case PUR-2025-00071
Attach your documentation. Keep a copy of everything you send.

3

Mail — For the Official Physical Record

Virginia State Corporation Commission
P.O. Box 1197, Richmond, VA 23218
Attn: Division of Public Utility Regulation
Write Case PUR-2025-00071 on the envelope and in your letter.

4

Phone — Only to Request the Email Address or Confirm Receipt

SCC Consumer Services: (804) 371-9141 or 1-800-552-7945 (toll-free in Virginia)
Do not rely on a phone call as your formal complaint. Use the phone only to get the correct email or confirm your written complaint was received.

📝 Complaint Template

Copy, Fill In, and Send

Replace the bracketed sections with your specific information. The more specific and documented, the stronger your complaint. Every complaint filed separately adds to the weight of evidence.

To: Virginia State Corporation Commission Division of Public Utility Regulation Re: Formal Complaint — Aqua Virginia, Inc. — Case No. PUR-2025-00071 Date: [TODAY'S DATE] Your Name: [YOUR FULL NAME] Address: [YOUR ADDRESS, CITY, STATE, ZIP] Account Number: [YOUR AQUA ACCOUNT NUMBER — on your bill] Phone: [YOUR PHONE] Email: [YOUR EMAIL] FORMAL COMPLAINT AGAINST AQUA VIRGINIA, INC. I am a customer of Aqua Virginia, Inc. in [your community — e.g., Lake Land'Or / Heritage / Lake Monticello]. I am filing this formal complaint in connection with Case No. PUR-2025-00071 and requesting that my documented experiences be included in the Commission's official record. WATER QUALITY ISSUES: [Describe specifically: "On [dates], my tap water was brown/discolored/had visible sediment. My whole-house filter was fully saturated after [X] days. Photos are attached. This is a regular occurrence, not an isolated event."] HEALTH IMPACTS: [Describe specifically: "My [child/family member] developed [eczema/rash/hair loss/skin irritation] after [timeframe]. We have been to [dermatologist/allergist/doctor] and are on [medication/treatment]. We believe the water quality is contributing to these health issues based on the Virginia Department of Health's confirmation of elevated iron and manganese levels in Aqua Virginia's system. Documentation attached."] APPLIANCE AND PROPERTY DAMAGE: [Describe specifically: "My [water heater/washing machine/dishwasher/plumbing fixtures] have [failed/been damaged/required replacement] which I attribute to the water quality. Repair receipts are attached. Total damage: $[amount]."] FINANCIAL HARM: [Describe specifically: "I spend approximately $[amount] per [month/year] on replacement water filters, bottled water, special soaps/lotions, and medical appointments related to water quality. My monthly bill has increased from $[old amount] to $[new amount]."] SERVICE FAILURES: [Describe specifically: "I experienced service failures on [Memorial Day 2025 / Labor Day 2025 / Thanksgiving 2025 / May 22, 2026 / other dates]. I received no advance notification. The May 22, 2026 pressure reduction event was confirmed by Aqua's own Disruption Event Viewer but no customer alert was issued."] BILLING CONCERNS: [If applicable: "My bill increased to $[amount] per month before the Commission has issued a final order in Case PUR-2025-00071. I am concerned rates are being applied before they have been officially approved."] I am requesting that: 1. My complaint be included in the formal record for Case PUR-2025-00071. 2. The Commission consider the documented health, financial, and property impacts on residents when determining the final rate order. 3. Aqua Virginia be required to remedy documented water quality issues as a condition of any rate approval. I am willing to provide additional documentation upon request. Respectfully, [YOUR SIGNATURE] [YOUR PRINTED NAME] [DATE]

Attach: bills, photos of water/filters, medical records, repair receipts, screenshots of Aqua's Disruption Event Viewer. Keep copies of everything.

⏱️ What Happens After You File

The Process

  • Your complaint enters the official SCC docket — it becomes part of the public record for Case PUR-2025-00071. Commissioners see it. Staff see it. Aqua's attorneys see it.
  • Aqua must respond — the SCC requires Aqua to respond to formal complaints. Their response is also on the record.
  • Volume matters — the more individual complaints filed, the stronger the pattern evidence. Every complaint is a separate documented incident. 50 complaints about eczema is different from 1.
  • Refund precedent exists — in the 2023-2024 Aqua Virginia rate case, when the SCC approved less than Aqua requested, customers received refunds with interest. Interim rates started January 26, 2026. If the final order approves less, documented complainants are in the best position to recover overpayments.
  • Final order is not yet issued — the window is still open. File before the Commission issues its final order in Case PUR-2025-00071 (expected October/November 2026).
