🔑 THE DISCLOSURE LAW — And Why It Matters

Virginia Code § 2.2-3114(A) explicitly names "members of the State Corporation Commission" as required to file annual financial disclosure statements — same category as the Governor, Supreme Court Justices, and circuit court judges.

What they must disclose: All personal financial interests including stock holdings, business interests, real estate, outside income, and positions in private entities.

Where the forms go: Filed with the Virginia Conflict of Interests and Ethics Advisory Council (ethics.dls.virginia.gov) by February 1 each year.

Public access: "All forms shall be maintained as public records for five years... Such forms shall be made public no later than six weeks after the filing deadline." — Va. Code § 2.2-3114(C).

What this means: Bagot's, Hudson's, and Towell's financial disclosure statements — listing every stock they own, every financial interest, every outside income source — are public records available right now. If any of them owns stock in Essential Utilities (WTRG), NextEra Energy (NEE), American Water Works (AWK), Digital Realty (DLR), Vanguard, BlackRock, or any regulated utility, it will be in those forms.

How to get them: See the FOIA section at the bottom of this page.

📋 How to Get the Financial Disclosures — Step by Step

The SCC Commissioners' Stock Holdings Are Public Record. Here's How to Get Them.

Step 1 — Virginia Ethics Council (Fastest Route)

The financial disclosure forms are filed with and maintained by the Virginia Conflict of Interests and Ethics Advisory Council. They are required to be made public no later than 6 weeks after February 1 — meaning they should be available by mid-March every year.

Contact: ethics.dls.virginia.gov | Phone: (804) 225-3138 | Email: ethics@dls.virginia.gov
Address: General Assembly Building, 201 N. 9th Street, Richmond, VA 23219

Ask for: "The annual financial disclosure statements filed by members of the Virginia State Corporation Commission — specifically Commissioners Kelsey Bagot, Samuel Towell, and Jehmal Hudson — for calendar years 2024 and 2025, pursuant to Virginia Code § 2.2-3114."

Step 2 — FOIA Request if Not Provided Voluntarily

If the Ethics Council does not provide the documents readily, file a formal FOIA request under Virginia Code § 2.2-3704. The forms are explicitly "maintained as public records" and "shall be made public" — there is no FOIA exemption for these specific filings.

FOIA request text: "Pursuant to the Virginia Freedom of Information Act, Va. Code § 2.2-3700 et seq., I request copies of all financial disclosure statements filed pursuant to Va. Code § 2.2-3114 by members of the Virginia State Corporation Commission, including Kelsey A. Bagot, Samuel T. Towell, and Jehmal T. Hudson, for the years 2024 and 2025."

Step 3 — Virginia SCC Clerk's Office: LLC Ownership Lookup

To find who is really behind "SpotsyTechnoCampus LLC," "RCK Digital Crossroads LLC," and other named entities, search the SCC's Clerk's Information System (CIS):

URL: scc.virginia.gov/clk
Search for: Each LLC name. The filing shows the registered agent and organizer — which often reveals the parent company. Free public search, no FOIA required.

Step 4 — Army Corps of Engineers Permit Files

All Army Corps public notices for data center permits in the region are publicly available:

URL: nao.usace.army.mil/Media/Public-Notices/
Already confirmed: NAO-2024-01108 (Mattameade, Caroline County — Amazon); NAO-2023-01952 (Amazon Tech Park, Spotsylvania). These files name the applicant, attorney, engineer, acreage, and environmental impact data.

Step 5 — SCC Docket Search: Active Proceedings

URL: scc.virginia.gov/docketsearch
Search "Aqua Virginia" — find PUR-2025-00071 (rate case) and monitor for the merger docket when filed.
Search "Essential Utilities" or "American Water" — the merger approval proceeding will be filed here.
Search "NextEra" or "Dominion" — the $66.8B merger docket will be filed here. Watch for it. File to intervene.
