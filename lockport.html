What "Cone of Depression" Means For Your Neighbors

When a large facility pumps massive groundwater volumes, it creates a funnel-shaped drop in the surrounding water table — pulling private wells and shallow municipal intakes dry within a 1–2 mile radius. In Lockport, where the aquifer is already under documented stress, this effect is amplified. Nearby residential wells can go dry within weeks. Drilling deeper costs homeowners $10,000–$25,000 out of pocket — with no reimbursement obligation on the developer.

ILLINOIS STATE LAW — YOUR LEGAL WEAPONS

⚖️ What Illinois Law Actually Gives You

Illinois Municipal Code
Full Local Zoning Authority
65 ILCS 5/11-13-1 through 5/11-13-25

Illinois municipalities have broad, independent authority to regulate land use through zoning ordinances — including classifying, restricting, and prohibiting specific industrial uses. Lockport is a home-rule municipality (population over 25,000), which gives it even broader authority than non-home-rule towns. Home-rule units can enact zoning regulations that exceed state minimums without special legislative approval.

USE TONIGHT

Ask: "Is the Star Innovation District currently zoned for data center use? If a Special Use Permit or rezoning is required, when will the public hearing be scheduled, and will residents receive formal notice?"

Illinois Home Rule Powers
Emergency Moratorium Authority
Illinois Constitution, Article VII, §6(a) — Home Rule

As a home-rule municipality, Lockport City Council can pass an emergency moratorium on industrial permits while conducting environmental and infrastructure studies. Aurora did exactly this — passing a 180-day moratorium on data centers and warehouses in September 2025. DeKalb County extended theirs under public pressure. Lockport can do the same tonight if the Council votes to act.

DEMAND THIS

Ask: "Will the City Council pass an emergency moratorium on data center permits for the Star Innovation District while these workshops are ongoing? That is standard practice — Aurora did it. DeKalb County did it. It protects the community while the City gathers information."

Illinois Environmental Protection Act
Environmental Impact Review Requirement
415 ILCS 5/1 et seq.

Facilities with significant water use, air emissions, or chemical storage are subject to Illinois EPA review. The former Texaco/Chevron refinery site has documented pre-existing contamination — any redevelopment requires a Phase I and Phase II Environmental Site Assessment. Data centers with diesel backup generators (required by all large facilities) are also subject to air permit requirements under IEPA rules.

USE IN PERMIT STAGE
Illinois Open Meetings Act
All Deliberations Must Be Public
5 ILCS 120/1 et seq.

Every deliberation, negotiation, and vote related to zoning, permitting, or annexation agreements for the Star Innovation District must occur in open public meetings with proper notice. Any decision made in a closed session or without proper notice is legally voidable. Non-disclosure agreements with developers do not override the Open Meetings Act for governmental actions.

WILL COUNTY LAW — LOCAL AUTHORITY

🏛️ Will County and City of Lockport — Local Legal Tools

Will County Land Use Department
County Zoning & Permitting Authority
Will County Zoning Ordinance; willcounty.gov/County-Offices/Economic-Development/Will-County-Land-Use-Department

Will County maintains independent zoning jurisdiction over unincorporated areas. For parcels within Lockport city limits, the City controls zoning — but County infrastructure permits (roads, utilities, stormwater) may be required. The Star Innovation District is within Lockport's borders, so the City Council's zoning vote is the primary gate.

Special Use Permit Process
Mandatory Public Hearing Before Any Data Center Permit
City of Lockport Zoning Code — requires SUP for heavy industrial uses

Data centers classified as heavy industrial require a Special Use Permit in most Illinois municipalities. A SUP requires: (1) a formal application, (2) public notice to all property owners within 250–500 feet, (3) a public hearing before the Zoning Board of Appeals, and (4) a separate vote by the full City Council. This creates two distinct veto points — the ZBA and the Council. Packing both with opposition is the goal.

KEY STRATEGY
Annexation Agreement
The Hidden Gate — Most Likely Vehicle
65 ILCS 5/11-15.1 — Illinois Municipal Annexation Authority

If any portion of the Star Innovation District is not yet within city limits, a developer may seek an annexation agreement — which is a contract between the City and the developer that bundles zoning, tax incentives, and infrastructure commitments. Annexation agreements require a public hearing AND a supermajority City Council vote. This is often where deals get locked in before residents know what happened. File a FOIA request specifically asking about any pending annexation agreement.

Village of Euclid v. Ambler Realty Co.
272 U.S. 365 (1926) — U.S. Supreme Court		 The foundational case for all American zoning law. The Supreme Court ruled that municipalities have the constitutional right to use zoning to protect residential areas from incompatible industrial uses. COMMUNITY WIN The entire legal basis for Lockport's right to say no to industrial development in or near residential zones. Cite this if anyone claims the city "can't" restrict data centers.
Hawthorne v. Village of Olympia Fields
Illinois Supreme Court, Docket No. 93462		 Illinois Supreme Court addressed whether a non-home-rule municipality can use statutory zoning authority to prevent specific industrial operations. Confirmed that zoning authority is broad and municipalities are not required to permit every industrial use. COMMUNITY WIN Lockport is home-rule (even stronger authority). This case confirms Illinois municipalities have wide discretion to restrict or deny industrial uses through zoning, even when a developer argues economic benefit.
Aurora, IL Data Center Moratorium
City of Aurora, Sept. 25, 2025 — Municipal ordinance		 Aurora City Council enacted a 180-day moratorium on new data center AND warehouse developments while conducting a comprehensive infrastructure and environmental review. PRECEDENT SET Direct Illinois precedent — same state, same legal framework. Lockport can do exactly this. Tonight, demand the Council pass a moratorium before any workshop recommendation is acted upon.

Pekin, IL Community Victory

Saline Township, MI Lawsuit

Newton County, GA — 2023–2024		 A data center was approved without a baseline groundwater study. After approval, residents discovered well depletion had already begun.
