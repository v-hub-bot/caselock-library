💧 What This Means for Your Well and the Rappahannock
  • The Cosner and Mattameade agreements use treated wastewater — but water that would otherwise return to the Rappahannock is instead permanently evaporated
  • The Rappahannock River was designated the 6th most endangered river in the US in April 2025 — data center pressure is a primary cited cause (Friends of the Rappahannock)
  • Caroline County received a DEQ permit (Nov 2025) to withdraw up to 9–13.9 million gallons/day from the Rappahannock River — driven by failing groundwater and 30-year demand projections that cited data center growth in the Carmel Church area. The Rappahannock Tribe filed an appeal Jan 2026. (Source: Bay Journal Jan 2025; Fredericksburg Free Press Nov 2024; DEQ Dec 2025)
  • For private well owners: high-volume industrial pumping nearby creates a documented USGS-confirmed "cone of depression" that can reverse groundwater flow and destabilize well pressure — this is the mechanism behind well disruptions documented nationally near data centers
  • The water agreements contain no binding cap limiting use to Amazon's stated 4.1% estimate — the maximum can be reached at any time

Why They Keep Approving Them

Spotsylvania Charges $1.25 Per $100. Neighbors Charge $4.50. That's Why They're All Coming Here.

🚨 The Tax Rate That Made Spotsylvania the Target

A signed economic development agreement locked in a data center equipment tax rate of $1.25 per $100 for Amazon for at least 20 years. Prince William charges $4.50. Fairfax and Loudoun charge $4.15. Spotsylvania charges less than one-third of what neighboring counties charge.

J.D. Keeling and Ricky Robinson in the comment thread identified this exactly. The math explains everything: data centers go where the rate is lowest. Spotsylvania's BOS made Spotsylvania the cheapest option in the entire region.

Source: Spotsylvania County Local Performance Agreement — dhcd.virginia.gov · Nick Minock Facebook post, confirmed against county documents
📊 The State Tax Break on Top of That
  • Virginia has exempted data centers from state sales and use tax (4.3%) since 2010. This now costs Virginia $1.9 billion per year in foregone revenue as of FY2025–2026 (Good Jobs First, February 20, 2026 — goodjobsfirst.org; Data Center Dynamics confirmed $1.6B for FY2025 in January 2026, subsequently revised upward)
  • Permanent jobs per facility: 2–30 people — security, maintenance, occasional technicians. Construction is temporary. The comment thread was right.
  • The 20-year lock-in means residents bear the infrastructure burden for two decades with no ability to renegotiate
  • Ricky Robinson's question about decommissioning is unanswered — no documented end-of-life plan exists for these facilities in Spotsylvania's agreements
It Can Be Won — Proof

25 Data Centers Were Stopped in 2025. Here's How.

✅ 25 Data Centers Cancelled in 2025 — Community Resistance Worked

Data center cancellations quadrupled in 2025 — from 6 in 2024 to 25 in 2025. At least 4.7 GW of planned capacity killed by community organizing. In September 2025, Microsoft abandoned a planned 244-acre data center after months of community resistance. (Heatmap Pro review of public records · Construction Dive · CRE Daily, Feb. 2026)

More than 140 local groups nationwide have blocked or delayed over $64 billion in data center investment — by showing up at meetings, not by hiring lobbyists. (Erin Brockovich report, April 28, 2026)

✅ Spotsylvania BOS Now Requires Special Use Permits — Feb 24, 2026

The BOS voted on February 24, 2026 to require SUPs for all new data center developments. They adopted: 300-foot residential buffers, 75-foot height limits, 400-foot setbacks. The RCK Digital project at Benchmark requires community approval through this process. The June 4 meeting is that process in action.

Source: Spotsylvania County "Guidelines for Data Center Development Adopted 2-24-2026" — spotsylvania.va.us/DocumentCenter/View/40711

🚨 The Rappahannock: 6th Most Endangered River in the US — April 2025

Friends of the Rappahannock confirmed this American Rivers designation in April 2025. Data center water extraction pressure is a primary cited cause. Before any new facility near you becomes operational. Test for: coliform bacteria, nitrates, iron, manganese, pH, arsenic, lead. Keep a dated sealed sample. Virginia Tech Well Water Program offers low-cost testing: wellwater.cals.vt.edu. If your water changes after construction ramps up — your dated baseline is the evidence that proves the before-state. Without it, you have no legal claim.
