🔗 The Documented Money Chain

From Utility Donations to Rate Approval — Step by Step

1
Utilities donate to Virginia legislators. Dominion Energy: $1.7M+ to Virginia legislators documented by the Energy and Policy Institute (energyandpolicy.org, reported by Utility Dive). Essential Utilities PAC: registered and active (OpenSecrets, C00340455). $27.7M in utility industry donations to Virginia legislators since 1996 (Virginia Conservation Network, vcnva.org).
2
Those same legislators elect SCC commissioners. Virginia SCC has 3 commissioners, elected by joint vote of the General Assembly — not by Virginia voters. Current commissioners: Kelsey A. Bagot (Chair), Jehmal T. Hudson, Samuel T. Towell. Six-year staggered terms. (Source: Virginia Constitution Article IX; SCC official website.)
3
Virginia law allows legislators to own utility stock AND vote on legislation that increases their wealth. This is documented by the Virginia Consumer Network. Two Virginia lawmakers proposed a bill to ban utility political contributions to legislators — because the conflict is that obvious. The bill did not pass. (Source: WRIC TV; Virginia Consumer Network.)
4
The SCC — appointed by donation-funded legislators — approves every Aqua rate increase. 8 rate increases since 2006. 8 approvals. Current case PUR-2025-00071: SCC approved a stipulation imposing an 18.7% rate hike on residents who cannot drink their water — and called it "reasonable."
5
The merger makes this permanent. The Essential Utilities / American Water merger requires SCC approval. The same three commissioners — appointed by donation-funded legislators — will decide whether a $40 billion combined entity takes control of Virginia's water. Without formal intervention now, residents have no seat at that table.

👥 The Human Cost — Documented

What This Looks Like on the Ground

Children — Eczema and Skin Conditions
Documented by Pam Taylor, lead community organizer: family members developed eczema and skin rashes attributed to water coming from taps. Eczema photos were collected and submitted as part of the SCC proceeding. Peer-reviewed literature (Journal of Dermatology 2018; Contact Dermatitis 2020) documents the link between elevated manganese and iron in water and skin irritation. VDH confirmed elevated levels. No court has adjudicated causation — this requires medical and legal expert review.
A Family Leaves Caroline County
A descendant of the Loving family — the family at the center of Loving v. Virginia, the landmark Supreme Court civil rights case — sold her home and left Caroline County due to the water crisis. This is documented community testimony. The symbolic and civil rights weight of this fact is not lost on attorneys who practice in this area.
Paying Twice — The Bottled Water Tax
JoAnne Watkins (Lake Monticello): "I can pay my bill and right after I am on my way to the grocery store to pick up cases of water." Residents pay Aqua's rate-increased bills AND pay separately for water safe enough to drink. This is a documentable economic harm — the double-cost of an unsafe monopoly service.
Filters Failing at 30 Days
Community-documented: water filters that should last 6 months are saturated and unusable within 30 days. This is a measurable, verifiable property-level economic harm that can be quantified in a damages calculation and supported by filter receipts and water quality logs.
Three Holiday Service Failures — 2025
Three documented service failures occurred in 2025 on or around major holidays. Documented by community members. Each failure is a potential Safe Drinking Water Act violation subject to citizen suit under 42 U.S.C. § 300j-8 if VDH notification requirements were not met.

How This Elevates Beyond a Standard Rate Complaint

Potential Legal Theories — Requires Attorney Verification
▶
Virginia Consumer Protection Act (VCPA) § 59.1-200(A): Unfair or deceptive trade practice — complaint suppression pattern, misrepresentation of service quality, billing for service not delivered at represented standard. Statutory damages available.
▶
Safe Drinking Water Act Citizen Suit — 42 U.S.C. § 300j-8: If VDH notification requirements were violated during holiday service failures or elevated contamination periods, citizen suit standing may exist. 60-day notice requirement before filing.
▶
Due Process / Rate Case Intervention: The structural conflict (utility donations → legislator appointments → SCC approval) can be raised as a due process objection in the merger proceeding. Must be filed as formal intervention in SCC Case PUR-2025-00071 or the merger docket.
▶
Environmental Justice / Civil Rights Frame: Displacement of a Loving v. Virginia descendant from Caroline County due to water quality failures in a predominantly rural, historically Black community creates an environmental justice argument that can attract federal attention (EPA Office of Environmental Justice, HUD, DOJ Civil Rights Division).
▶
Class Action Potential: All affected Lake Land'Or and Caroline County Aqua customers share identical harm — contaminated water, elevated bills, suppressed complaint records. Class certification requires attorney leadership but the community base is already organized and documented.
▶
Merger Intervention — Kill Shot Window: The Essential Utilities / American Water merger requires SCC approval. Intervening as a formal party — with this documented record — creates binding obligations on Aqua before the merger closes. After close, leverage disappears. This window is open now and closes with the merger.
