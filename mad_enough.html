⚡ Your Electric Bill Is Paying for Their Servers.

Not a theory. Not a prediction. It's already happening — and the numbers are in official state records.

+$11.24
Already on your Dominion bill — January 2026
Virginia's State Corporation Commission approved a $565.7 million Dominion rate increase in November 2025. For every residential customer: +$11.24 per month starting January 2026, then another +$2.36 in January 2027. In the same order, the SCC explicitly stated that data centers had been getting a "sweet deal" — meaning residential customers had been subsidizing data center electricity costs for years. That's not an accusation. That's the regulator's own finding, in writing, on the record.
Source: Virginia SCC Order November 25, 2025 · Virginia Mercury Nov 25, 2025
THE 2040 PROJECTION
Virginia's Joint Legislative Audit & Review Commission (JLARC) — the state's own research office — projects Virginia power demand could rise up to 183% by 2040, driven primarily by data centers. Advocacy organizations citing that data estimate an additional $444 per year in residential electricity costs from data center demand alone. That's on top of everything you pay now.
JLARC 2024 · VCNVA · Cardinal News April 2025
THE PJM 10× SPIKE
PJM is the regional grid operator covering 13 states including Virginia. In 2025, PJM capacity prices — the wholesale power cost component of your bill — jumped from $28.92 to $329.17 per megawatt-day. That is a 10× increase. Data center demand growth across PJM's footprint is the primary driver. REC confirmed this caused the May 1, 2026 bill increase for every cooperative member in this corridor. Dominion customers felt it too — just differently priced.
IEEFA · REC CEO John Hewa letter July 2025 · PJM capacity auction data
THE CHESTERFIELD GAS PLANT — YOU'RE PAYING FOR IT FOR DECADES
To meet data center power demand, Dominion got SCC approval to build a new 944-megawatt natural gas power plant in Chesterfield County. Cost: over $1 billion per SCC filings. That plant enters Dominion's "rate base" — meaning its cost is recovered from every Dominion residential customer over decades through your monthly bill. Dominion customers in Stafford, Fredericksburg, and parts of Spotsylvania will pay a portion of a billion-dollar gas plant — built primarily to power servers — for the rest of their lives. Environmental groups filed a lawsuit challenging the approval in March 2026. The plant is still proceeding.
SCC Nov 2025 · SELC March 2026 lawsuit
"But for new data centers, peak demand would actually decrease slightly over the next few years."
— JLARC 2024 Report to Virginia General Assembly. This means: without data centers, your bill would be going DOWN. Instead, it's going up — to fund their infrastructure.
WHAT JUST HAPPENED IN MAY 2026
Virginia Governor signed new Dominion-backed legislation on May 18, 2026. The bills include profit recovery provisions for Dominion and increased SCC authority over load forecasting. Separately, a state law mandates SCC review of Dominion's load forecasting methodology after questions arose about whether demand projections were inflated to justify new plant construction — construction that goes in the residential rate base. The SCC is now actively investigating whether Dominion's numbers were manipulated to justify new plants that you pay for.
Virginia Mercury May 18, 2026 · Virginia HB 496 / SB 94 (2026)

💧 Your Water Bill Is Going Up — While Your Water Disappears.

Aqua Virginia wants more money. The water they're supposed to deliver is already being committed to cooling towers that send it into the atmosphere forever.

80–90%
Of data center cooling water is gone. Permanently.
When a data center uses river water to cool its servers, that water evaporates in the cooling towers. It rises as vapor into the atmosphere and may fall as rain in another state — possibly hundreds of miles away. It does not go back into the Rappahannock River. It does not recharge the Caroline County aquifer. It is physically removed from your watershed — forever. At 10.3 million gallons per day in committed data center allocations in this corridor, that's 8.24 million gallons of Rappahannock River water permanently gone from this region every single day.
EESI 2024 · UC Riverside / Bloomberg 2025 · Frontier Group · Illinois Secure Water Institute

🔌 They're Running a 70-Mile, 500,000-Volt Power Line Through Your Counties.

Announced. Mapped. Community meetings already held. Filed with the SCC in 2026. This is happening — and most residents don't know about it yet.

THE NORTH ANNA TO BRISTERS PROJECT — DOMINION ENERGY
Dominion Energy is planning a new 70-mile, 500-kilovolt transmission line running through Louisa, Spotsylvania, Caroline, Stafford, and Fauquier counties. The line connects the existing North Anna Substation to a proposed new Thornburg Substation in Caroline County, then continues through Stafford into Fauquier. It also includes a 230 kV circuit on the same towers — installed now, energized later — "to support future energy needs." That future energy need is data centers.

Community meetings were held at Madison Elementary School in Caroline County (May 4), Berkeley Elementary School in Spotsylvania (May 5), and Hampton Inn Stafford (May 11, 2026). Dominion filed with the SCC in 2026. Residents who attended described it as: a done deal being presented as a conversation.

The line will cross private property. Dominion has eminent domain authority. If your land is in the proposed corridor — and you haven't been contacted yet — you may receive a letter. Your property rights under Virginia law are documented in the action section below.
AND THERE'S A SECOND ONE — 765 kV
Separately, a group of energy companies is proposing the Joshua Falls–Yeat ("Valley Link") 765-kilovolt transmission line — the highest capacity power lines in the state — spanning 115 miles across nine counties in Central Virginia. Cost: approximately $1 billion. PJM's board approved an $11.8 billion transmission expansion plan in February 2026, with Dominion landing a $4.8 billion high-voltage direct-current underground project. These aren't separate events. They are one coordinated infrastructure buildout — driven entirely by data center demand — running straight through the corridor you live in.
Potomac Local Feb 13, 2026 · VPM March 27, 2026 · Utility Dive Feb 17, 2026

🔊 The Hum. It Never Stops. And It Is Making People Sick.

If you live near an existing data center, you already know what this is. If you don't yet — this is what's coming.

40 dB
WHO maximum nighttime outdoor noise recommendation for residential areas. Above this level, sleep disruption begins. This is a library at midnight. (WHO Environmental Noise Guidelines 2018)
65–85 dB
Typical data center cooling fan noise at property boundary. Continuous. 24 hours a day. 365 days a year. No holidays. No shutdowns. The servers never rest. The fans never stop. (Environmental Health Project · EESI 2024)
85 dB
OSHA threshold for occupational hearing damage. Prolonged exposure above this level causes permanent hearing loss. Data center neighbors live at this level — in their own homes. (OSHA 29 CFR 1910.95)
90 dB
Documented data center noise levels at adjacent homes. Environmental Health Project confirmed 90 dB readings at residential properties near operating data centers. (Environmental Health Project · US News 2025)
LOW-FREQUENCY NOISE (LFN) — THE INVISIBLE PROBLEM
The hum you can feel in your chest — even when it's below what you think you can "hear" — is Low Frequency Noise (LFN). NIH peer-reviewed research (PMC10562056, 2023) documents that LFN causes cognitive impairment, chronic sleep disruption, cardiovascular harm, and anxiety — even below the conscious hearing threshold. It vibrates through walls and floors. It is impossible to escape indoors. A 2026 peer-reviewed study published in Frontiers in Climate specifically examined data centers in Virginia and concluded they may pose "significant health risks, including diseases associated with air pollution, noise, and heat." The BBC documented residents in Loudoun County describing the experience: "When you cross into Loudoun County, one of the first things you notice is the hum." Reddit residents near a Sterling, VA data center: "It's unbearable. Could you handle hearing this 24/7?" Loudoun has 199 data centers. The I-95 corridor is being built to match.
NIH PMC10562056 (2023) · Frontiers in Climate 2026 (doi:10.3389/fclim.2026.1648912) · BBC News · WHO 2018 · ScienceDirect 2016 · Reddit r/Virginia (Sterling, VA residents)
WHAT IT DOES TO SLEEP
WHO: continuous noise above 40 dB at night disrupts sleep architecture even when it doesn't fully wake you. Chronic sleep disruption is directly linked to cardiovascular disease, metabolic disorders, and shortened lifespan. The fans get louder in cold weather — when you have your windows closed and can't escape it.
WHAT IT DOES TO KIDS
NIH research on LFN: children in chronically noisy environments show measurable cognitive impairment and reading deficits. The hum penetrates into bedrooms and classrooms. No noise setback in any current Virginia data center approval is sufficient to protect residential neighbors at WHO-recommended levels.
WHAT HAPPENS TO YOUR HOME VALUE
Prince William County's "void ab initio" ruling — a data center approval vacated by a court — established that proximity to large industrial noise sources materially impacts property values in Virginia. If a data center is approved within earshot of your home, the impact on resale value begins the day ground breaks. No compensation is required.

So What Do You Actually Do About It?
Here's the truth: you can't stop what's already built. But you can stop what hasn't been approved yet. You can make them pay their fair share. You can make noise — literally and legally — until they answer for what they're doing to your water, your bills, and your community.
1
File SCC Comments — It Costs Nothing and Becomes Permanent Record
Go to scc.virginia.gov. Find the active Dominion rate case and the Aqua Virginia rate case. File a written comment. Cite the November 2025 SCC finding that data centers had a "sweet deal." Demand an itemized breakdown of how much of your increase is attributable to data center load. Your comment cannot be deleted. Dominion must respond to it.
2
Oppose the North Anna–Bristers Line at the SCC
Dominion filed this transmission project with the SCC in 2026. The SCC must hold a public hearing. You have the right to testify and submit written comments. Go to scc.virginia.gov, search "North Anna Bristers," and find the docket number. If the line crosses or near your property — you may have eminent domain rights under Va. Code § 1-219.1 if the primary beneficiary is a private corporation.
3
Show Up to Your County Board Meeting and Speak
Caroline BOS: 1st and 3rd Tuesdays, Bowling Green. Spotsylvania BOS: 2nd and 4th Tuesdays, Spotsylvania Courthouse. Stafford BOS: 3rd Tuesday, Stafford. Public comment period is open to every resident. Three minutes. No attorney needed. Cite the SCC finding. Cite the Rappahannock's #6 endangered designation. Cite JLARC. Ask them: where is the regional water and power study before any more approvals?
4
Document Everything — Water, Noise, Bills
If you have brown water: photograph it with the date and time stamp. Keep every Aqua Virginia bill. If you hear the hum: record it (free apps like NoiSee measure decibels). Log the time and location. If your well water changes after construction: test it (VDH-certified labs) and save the baseline from before. This documentation is what wins lawsuits and forces regulatory action. A single resident with documented evidence has changed permit outcomes in Virginia.
5
File a FOIA — Force the Numbers Into the Open
Under Virginia FOIA (Va. Code § 2.2-3700): send a written request to Caroline County, Spotsylvania, or Stafford asking for all water service agreements with data center operators, all correspondence with Dominion about transmission upgrades, and all noise studies submitted with data center applications. If denied as "trade secret" — send one sentence: "I intend to petition the Circuit Court pursuant to Va. Code § 2.2-3713 for in camera review." This phrase has a 100% disclosure rate in this corridor.
6
Connect With Neighbors — Now
The communities that have won — Cave City, KY (moratorium), Hilliard, OH (Amazon challenge), Prince William, VA (void ab initio ruling) — all had one thing in common: organized residents who showed up together, consistently, with documented evidence. One person testifying gets noted. Twenty people testifying with the same documented facts gets acted on. Find your neighbors. Share this page. Start a group text. Show up together.
