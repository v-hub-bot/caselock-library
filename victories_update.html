New Wins, New Fights:
Festus MO, Hanover VA, Indiana, and the Recall Playbook

A Missouri town ousted every council member who approved a $6 billion data center — one week after the vote. Hanover County Virginia approved a 1,200-acre campus over massive community opposition. Indiana counties are fighting back. The pattern is clear. The playbook is documented.

🏆 FESTUS, MISSOURI — The Most Important Victory of 2026

Every Council Member Who Approved a $6 Billion Data Center Was Voted Out — One Week Later

On March 30, 2026, the Festus, Missouri city council approved a development agreement with CRG Clayco for a likely hyperscale data center on 360 acres just north of Highway 67. One week later, on April 9, 2026, every incumbent council member who supported the development was voted out of office.

  • Population of Festus: approximately 14,000 people — this was a small town making a massive statement
  • Residents also collected signatures for a recall of Mayor Sam Richards at polling places on election day
  • In neighboring Pacific, MO: Mayor Heather Filley — who supported a separate proposed data center — also lost her reelection to a pro-transparency candidate
  • CRG Clayco's previous proposal in St. Charles, MO ended with a yearlong moratorium
  • A Sunshine Law (FOIA) request revealed city officials had referred to the data center opposition group as "uneducated" — that document became a flashpoint
The lesson: Transparency was the winning issue — not just opposition to the data center itself. City officials calling opponents "uneducated" in internal emails, combined with the perception that the council was conducting business secretly with the developer, created the political conditions for a sweep. The same dynamic is present in every community where this fight is happening.

⚠️ HANOVER COUNTY, VIRGINIA — The Fight Still Active

Mountain Road Tech Park: 1,200 Acres Approved. A Second 430-Acre Campus Moving Forward. 7 Total Projects.

  • Hanover Technology Park: Approved March 2024 — the first major campus in the county. 1,200 acres north of the Town of Ashland. Approved by Board of Supervisors.
  • Mountain Road Tech Park (430 acres): Developer is Tract — a data center developer already active in Hanover. Planning Commission voted 6-1 to recommend approval in April 2026. The lone "no" vote: Ashland Commissioner Alan Abbott. Now goes to Board of Supervisors for final decision.
  • Total in county: Instagram documentation from local advocates (April 2026) confirms 7 data center projects have or are attempting to develop in Hanover County — approved, pending, or in planning.
  • Community concern: "Older people with health issues and families who have children with asthma will have to move if the Mountain Road Tech Park is built." (Stop Hanover Data Centers, May 15, 2026)
  • The hashtag movement: #growtomatoesnotdatacenters — the elderly woman in the "Grow Tomatoes" t-shirt testifying at the Board hearing became a viral image with 5.8K likes and 1.1K shares
This fight is still open. The Mountain Road Tech Park (430 acres, developer Tract) received planning commission recommendation but the Board of Supervisors has not voted yet. If you are a Hanover County resident, this is your window. Contact the Hanover County Board of Supervisors and attend the public hearing. The Festus, Missouri model — electoral accountability — applies directly here.

🗺️ Indiana + Hancock County — The Midwest Fight

Google Is Moving In. Communities Are Organized. Some Are Winning.

  • Hancock County, Indiana: Active community fight — Citizens Action Coalition held a special meeting on data center siting on May 18, 2026 (3 days ago as of this report). The county is forming an advisory committee on data center standards. A community meeting was held at Greenfield High School to hear input on a proposed data center planned unit development.
  • Kym SmithSoderholm's comment in the Hanover post tagged "Brad Winner, Hancock County Conservative Action" — confirming there is an organized conservative opposition group in Hancock County specifically. Conservative opposition to data centers in Indiana mirrors the bipartisan pattern documented nationally (55% Republican / 45% Democrat among opposing officials, per Data Center Watch).
  • At least 4 Indiana counties had turned away data centers as of September 2025 (Indiana Facebook community documentation)
  • Indiana opposition is winning: WFYI (Indiana Public Media) confirmed October 2025 — "organized community opposition is making tech giants rethink" Indiana data center plans. Tax breaks and affordable land brought them in; organized opposition is complicating expansion.
  • New Indiana legislation: A 2026 Indiana bill would change where data centers can be built and how much public input communities get (WTHR13 reporting)
Sources: Citizens Action Coalition of Indiana YouTube (May 18, 2026 special meeting); Hancock County Indiana government (advisory committee formation); WTHR13; WFYI Indiana Public Media October 28, 2025; Facebook data center opposition groups.
