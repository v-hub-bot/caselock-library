⚠️ Statement of Correction — May 23, 2026

We Got It Wrong.
Here Is What Happened.

This library exists to document verified facts. When errors occur — even unintentional ones — we correct them publicly, explain exactly how they happened, and implement systems to prevent them from happening again. That is the standard we hold ourselves to.

📢 Direct Statement

To Supervisor Jeff Black and to Aqua Virginia

Two specific errors appeared in this library that named Supervisor Jeff Black in ways that were not supported by verified primary sources. We are correcting the record publicly and without qualification.

To Supervisor Jeff Black: Two statements were attributed to you that could not be verified against any published news article, official transcript, SCC docket entry, or documented public record. One attributed a personal account of animal deaths to you by name. The other characterized you as a "named witness to perjury." Neither characterization had a verifiable primary source. Both have been removed. The only statement attributed to you in this library is your verified, published quote from the April 28, 2026 SCC hearing — "Your mission has failed" — which was reported by CBS 6 / WTVR and is a matter of public record. We regret that inaccurate characterizations appeared under your name.

To Aqua Virginia: One section heading in this library read "Aqua's Witness Lies." This was editorial characterization that went beyond what this library is designed to document. A community member's personal opinion expressed at a public hearing is documented community testimony. It is not a legal finding. No court has determined that any Aqua Virginia representative committed perjury or made false statements under oath. That language has been corrected. Aqua Virginia's documented rate case history, SCC filings, and public statements remain in the record — attributed to their sources and framed as evidence for regulatory review, not as conclusions of wrongdoing.

🔍 Exactly What Was Wrong — And Why

The Three Errors — Root Cause Identified

Error 1 — Animal Deaths Attributed to Jeff Black
The library contained a quote: "I lost 2 kittens because I wasn't thinking and gave them my tap water... they passed in 2 days." — attributed by name to Supervisor Jeff Black.

How it happened: This account was shared in community conversation and described as coming from the lead organizer. Jeff Black is identified throughout this record as the lead organizer. Without a primary source (a public post, video, or transcript), the name was placed on the quote by inference — not by verification. That is not the standard this library requires.

Current status: Removed. The event is documented as attributed community testimony with no individual named, pending a verifiable primary source.
Error 2 — "Named Witness to Perjury"
Jeff Black's profile card in the library included the phrase: "Named witness to perjury."

How it happened: The page section heading read "Aqua's Witness Lies." The quote in that section was attributed to Jeff Black (the first error above). Combining: Jeff Black's name + section heading about lying under oath + perjury statute citation = the characterization "named witness to perjury" was produced. No source ever used that phrase. It was generated by combining three separate things that should never have been combined.

Current status: Removed. Replaced with his verified CBS6/WTVR quote.
Error 3 — SCC "Five-Member Commission Appointed by the Governor"
The library stated: "The same five-member commission appointed by the Governor decides all of it."

How it happened: The Virginia Constitution authorizes up to five commissioners — but the SCC has operated with three since 1919. The Governor appointed commissioners from 1903 to 1919 only. Both the historical maximum and the historical appointment method were written as current fact. Neither is accurate today.

Current status: Corrected. The SCC has exactly 3 commissioners (Bagot, Towell, Hudson), elected by joint vote of the General Assembly to 6-year terms. Source: Virginia Constitution Article IX; SCC official website.

🔒 How This Doesn't Happen Again

New Standard — Effective Immediately

📋
Every named individual claim requires a primary source before publication. A primary source is: a published news article, an official SCC or court transcript, a government website, a public video, or a document the named person submitted themselves. Community conversation alone is not sufficient to place a specific quote or characterization under a named person's identity.
📤
Community members submit their own testimony, in their own words, under their own name or anonymously. This library does not speak for community members. Community members speak for themselves — through the Community Evidence Board, where they can upload documents, photos, bills, and written statements directly.
🔍
All statistics and structural facts are verified against two independent sources before publication. The SCC commissioner count error came from reading a historical figure as a current one. Every number is now verified against the primary source — not an article about the primary source.
⚖️
Legal characterizations (perjury, fraud, lying) are never placed on named individuals. These are conclusions for courts and prosecutors. This library documents evidence. Community opinions expressed at public hearings are documented as attributed community testimony — not adopted as CaseLock findings.
🔄
When errors are found, they are corrected publicly with full explanation. This page is permanent. It will not be deleted. Accountability for errors is part of the record.
📌 Note on This Library's Purpose

CaseLock Intelligence exists to organize verified, documented public records so that communities facing monopoly utility issues and infrastructure disputes can find and use that information. It is not a legal filing. It is not an investigation. It does not make legal findings.

The community members who share their experiences here are speaking for themselves, from their own lives, with their own documents. That is the foundation this library is built on.
