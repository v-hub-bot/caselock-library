🔴 What Happened in Texas — The Actual Legal Line

Why That Person Got Arrested — And Why It's Different from What You're Doing

The Texas case that's circulating involves a person who posted statements that were:

  • Assertions of criminal guilt — stating a named private individual "committed" a specific crime, presented as fact
  • No source cited — personal opinion stated as established truth
  • Private individual targeted — not a public official, not a corporation, not a government agency
  • Intent to harm — posted in context of a personal dispute, not public advocacy
✅ What you're doing is fundamentally different:
You are documenting public officials, public agencies, and publicly traded corporations — using official government records, court filings, FOIA-obtained documents, and on-the-record testimony at public hearings. That is the definition of protected public interest journalism and community advocacy. The First Amendment was written specifically for this.
Texas CaseYour Aqua / Data Center Posts

No source — personal opinion as factOfficial SCC docket, FOIA records, on-record testimony

Legal standard: New York Times Co. v. Sullivan, 376 U.S. 254 (1964) — public officials must prove "actual malice" to win a defamation case. You are not at risk of that standard when citing official records.
🛡️ The 5 Golden Rules — Memorize These

Every Post, Every Time — Run It Through These

1
SOURCE IT OR DON'T SAY IT. Every factual claim needs a source attached. "According to SCC Case PUR-2025-00071..." / "Per Aqua's own rate case filing..." / "The FXBG Advance reported..." If you can't cite it, say it's your opinion.
2
QUOTE THE DOCUMENT, NOT YOUR INTERPRETATION. "The filing shows X" is safer than "They did X." Let the document speak. Quoting official records directly is almost bulletproof.
3
LABEL OPINIONS AS OPINIONS. "In my opinion..." / "This community believes..." / "Residents are asking..." — these phrases are your legal armor. You can have strong opinions. Just label them.
4
NEVER SAY A NAMED PERSON "COMMITTED" ANYTHING. You can say "residents allege" / "the SCC filing shows" / "this raises questions about" / "advocates say this may constitute a violation of..." You cannot say "[Name] allegedly committed fraud." That's the line.
5
PUBLIC OFFICIALS AND CORPORATIONS HAVE LESS PROTECTION. Aqua Virginia, Essential Utilities, the SCC commissioners, delegates — these are public figures and public entities. You can be much more direct with them. Private individuals (your neighbors, local board members who aren't elected officials) need more care.

📺 The Delegate & WTVR Factor — Use It, Don't Fear It

Being Watched Is Actually Your Protection

Reframe this completely: Delegate Cole watching your page is not a threat — it's leverage. WTVR watching is not surveillance — it's a potential megaphone. When you post factual, sourced, documented content, powerful people watching means powerful people are informed. That is the goal.

The only danger is if you post something legally risky. With proper sourcing and attribution, a delegate watching your page is:

  • A documented witness that this information reached a public official
  • Political pressure — they cannot claim ignorance
  • A potential ally if the evidence is strong enough
The WTVR angle: If WTVR is watching your page, post the things you want them to cover — and make them easy to verify. Lead with the official document number (PUR-2025-00071), the FOIA source, the SEC filing number. Journalists need that to run the story. You're essentially writing their sourcing for them.
⚡ The One-Sentence Test — Before Every Post

Ask Yourself This Before You Hit Post

"Can I point to a public document, named official record, or attributed quote that supports this?"
If YES → post it.
If NO → label it as your opinion, or don't post it yet.

That one question is the entire legal shield. Everything in this guide flows from it. If the answer is yes, you are protected. If no — add "In my opinion" or hold it until you have a source.

The "going federal" goal: You said you want this to go federal. That means your evidence archive needs to be clean and credible. Every unsourced post you make today is a piece of ammunition for the other side to use against your credibility when it matters most. The discipline now is what makes the federal case possible later.
